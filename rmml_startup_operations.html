RMML Startup Operations

RMML startup team was involved in developing surgical robots for orthopedics and grew out of the Robots and Medical Mechatronics Laboratory (RMML) project at National Taiwan University (NTU) in 2014.
The mission of the team was to deliver innovative technology for the healthcare industry.

Product

The first product is an intelligent surgical system, which consists of navigation software and an intelligent handheld robot. The system can achieve auto-tracking of the surgical tool and tremor suppression from the operator, thus provides unique features of ease-of-use for minimally invasive spine surgery.

My Role

As an operations and project manager, my responsibilities were:

  • Raised money for the startup. Presented to venture capitalists as well as angel investors including Ministry of Science and Technology, Johnson & Johnson, and PwC in Taiwan
  • Worked with the founder, a vice president at PwC, and business managers at Taiwan Supra Integration and Incubation Center (Si2C) to establish step-by-step business strategic and financial planning
  • Led the team to participate in startup competitions and wrote business proposals
  • Created a project management process for the R&D team of 6 engineers and increased productivity; started defining and prioritizing deliverables
  • Engaged with stakeholders including intellectual property, management, and regulatory consulting firms
  • Negotiated technology transfer and licensing agreements with NTU


Metrics

  • Raised $0.4 million in a seed round by selected to join Taiwan Supra Integration and Incubation Center (Si2C)
  • Selected as the top 3 presenters in the semi-final FITI competition; the team placed among the top 20 teams and won a $4,000 cash award
  • Implemented project management framework and design control process
