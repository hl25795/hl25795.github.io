Master's Project

To improve startup successful rates, one suggestion is to devote effort to develop management skills and use effective project management approaches. The project is divided into two parts: the first part investigates present cases and literature reviews, and the second part creates a customized project management framework applied on a Taiwan startup case based on the first part review.

RMML startup team is a case in this paper. It is a Taiwan robotic surgery startup and faces challenges that other new firms may encounter. By understanding the situation and resources of RMML team, a plan template is provided. The plan focuses on two of three project management functions, the definition and the planning steps, lets the team see a big picture of the project. Each step has its purpose. When doing project definition, issues will be framed. It is a way to get the team on the same page and speak the same language. The project planning step, separately, analyzes details through breaking down the activities and links the relationships of each task. Project manager and the team could allocate resources and monitor the schedule based on the plan.

In sum, the project gives a good chance to put knowledge into practice with a real case study. The project is intended for entrepreneurs who start to run new companies and find it hard to run businesses effectively and efficiently.

  • Advisor: Bruce McCann Ph.D.
  • Reference: Project Presentation
  • Date: May 2018

Undergraduate Thesis

The purpose of the thesis is to analyze human intents in steering a passive walker equipped with an inertial measurement unit (IMU). A passive walker with this sensor had been installed and tested. Quantified data from IMU were collected and then used to establish a corresponding relationship to the human intents and motion.

My laboratory partners-Yu-Hong Chen and Siou-Jing Lin-used different sensors equipped in the same passive walker to collect data. One paper we published is to compare two methods for the measurement: 6-axis force/torque sensor and pressure sensors. Preliminary result shows that a passive walker with low-cost pressure sensors can still provide human intent assessments as well as the one with high price force/torque sensor.

  • Advisor: Prof. Ping-Lang Yen
  • Reference: IEEE Xplore
  • Date: June 2013


Mechatronics Project

The final demonstration on Mechatronics & Laboratory course is to present one Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) controlled mechanism designed by student teams. Four to five students form a group to devise a project. My team designed a rain detection mechanism to avoid items like clothes getting wet in the rains when people are not home or around there. We used a simple water detector and a vibration sensor to detect whether it is rain or not. The signal output of the sensors connected to PLC by customized circuit board, and PLC connected with an auto open/close umbrella. Our idea is that when drops of water falling onto the surface of sensors will change voltage and make a different signal output. Then, the signal will trigger the umbrella to open through PLC. We demonstrated a prototype on final demo day.

  • Lecturer: Prof. Jui-jen Chou
  • Course: Mechatronics & Laboratory
  • Date: December 2012


The 2012 International Symposium on Robotics

Plant factories project was a co-development work between different laboratories in our department. In my laboratory, We demonstrated the role of a mobile robot arm its surveillance on the concentration of nutrition solution during growing process of vegetable. The total yield and quality of vegetable production can be greatly raised by accurate control of the nutrition concentration for each plant according to different growing stages of the vegetable. The mobile robot arm is designed to move to specific planting bed, monitor the local concentration of nutrition for individual vegetable, and even perform on-site adjustment of the concentration of nutrition solution. I presented our paper in the 2012 international symposium on robotics.

  • Reference: ISR 2012
  • Date: August 2012
